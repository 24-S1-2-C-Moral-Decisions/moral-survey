Background Knowledge:

What is Reddit and its communities?

Reddit is an American social news aggregation, content rating, and discussion website. Registered users submit content to the site such as links, text posts, images, and videos, which are then voted up or down by other members. Posts are organized by subject into user-created boards called "communities", each focusing on a specific topic or theme.

What is the r/AmItheAsshole community?

r/AmItheAsshole, abbreviated as AITA, is a Reddit community where users post about their real-world interpersonal conflicts and receive judgment from other Reddit users.

This community allows users to solicit and express opinions about whether the actions of people in specific scenarios are appropriate, especially the actions of Original Poster, the person reporting the situation.

We categorize 2 Primary Judgments in the r/AmItheAsshole Community:

1. YA: Yes, the Individual reporting the situation is an Asshole.

It combines two types of judgments from the AITA community:

You are The Asshole (YTA): the Individual reporting the situation is at fault.

Everyone Sucks Here (ESH): all parties involved in the situation are at fault.

2. NA: No, the Individual reporting the situation is Not an Asshole.

It combines two types of judgments from the AITA community:

You are Not The Asshole (NTA): the Individual reporting the situation is not at fault in the situation.

No Asshole Here (NAH): none of the parties involved are at fault.

1.1 What is Controversy?

Controversy reflects how split people's judgements are in a situation:

High controversy means people's opinions are split almost evenly among 2 judgments (YA, NA).

Low controversy means a clear consensus among the whole population, or say people's opinions leaning to one of the judgments.
